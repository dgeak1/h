1. Purpose & Attendance

The meeting was convened by the Co-Staff Adviser, Mr. Fasoro, to address internal board issues and procedural lapses. Attendees: Co-staff Adviser, President, Speaker, General Secretary, Welfare Director, Assistant General Secretary, PRO, Social Director, and Financial Secretary.

2. Fresher Night & Proposed Guest
  • Guest Selection: The proposal to feature DJ Chicken was debated. The board deemed this "low impact" for students and potentially harmful to the faculty's image.
  • Classism Concerns: High ticket pricing was identified as a barrier that would create unnecessary "classism" among the student body.
  • Unauthorized Voting: An award voting link was circulated and payments were collected without the knowledge of the President, Assistant, or Staff Adviser.
3. Disciplinary Conduct & Character

The Adviser addressed the rude and unprofessional tone used by the Social Director during a recent phone call. It was emphasized that character and respect are vital executive values. The board noted that "insubordination and hooliganism" are unacceptable, and issues must be approached calmly to avoid creating chaos.

4. Executive Roles & Approval Hierarchy
The Hierarchy of Action:
  1. Individual Executive initiates a proposal.
  2. Board consensus and Presidential validation (required for all public events).
  3. Presentation to the Staff Adviser for consideration.
  4. Final approval by the Dean.
Note: The General Secretary’s role in writing official letters (e.g., the cancellation letter) was clarified as a mandatory administrative duty.
5. Resolutions & Mediation
  • Social Director: Pleaded that no disrespect was intended toward the Adviser; his frustrations were directed at the President.
  • Speaker’s Verdict: Stressed that no event can hold without following the proper hierarchy. Working in "isolation" is a failure of transparency.
  • Final Action: The Social Director is required to submit a formal letter of apology. The meeting concluded with a plea for peace from the Financial Secretary.
